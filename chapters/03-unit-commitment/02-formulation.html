Formulation

Here we present the mathematical formulation of the unit commitment problem solved by the model.

  • D.A. Tejada-Arango, S. Lumbreras, P. Sánchez-Martín, and A. Ramos “Which Unit-Commitment Formulation is Best? A Systematic Comparison” IEEE Transactions on Power Systems 35 (4): 2926-2936, Jul 2020 10.1109/TPWRS.2019.2962024

Indices

Symbol Description
\(n\) Load level
\(\nu\) Time step. Duration of each load level (e.g., 2 h, 3 h)
\(g\) Generator (thermal or hydro unit or ESS)
\(t\) Thermal unit
\(e\) Energy Storage System (ESS)

Parameters

They are written in capital letters.

Demand

Symbol Description Unit
\(D_n\) Demand GW
\(DUR_n\) Duration of each load level h
\(CENS\) Cost of energy not served. Value of Lost Load (VoLL) €/MWh

Operating reserves

Symbol Description Unit
\(UR_n, DR_n\) Upward and downward operating reserves GW

Generation system

Symbol Description Unit
\(\underline{CP}_g, \overline{GP}_g\) Minimum load and maximum output of a generator GW
\(\overline{GC}_g\) Maximum consumption of an ESS GW
\(CF_g, CV_g\) Fixed and variable cost of a generator. Variable cost includes fuel, O&M and emission cost €/h, €/MWh
\(RU_t, RD_t\) Ramp up and ramp down of a thermal unit MW/h
\(TU_t, TD_t\) Minimum uptime and downtime of a thermal unit h
\(CSU_g, CSD_g\) Startup and shutdown cost of a committed unit M€
\(\tau_e\) Characteristic duration of the ESS (e.g., 24 h, 168 h, 672 h -for monthly-) h
\(EF_e\) Round-trip efficiency of the pump/turbine cycle of a hydro power plant or charge/discharge of a battery p.u.
\(I_e\) Capacity of an ESS (e.g., hydro power plant) GWh
\(EI_{ne}\) Energy inflows of an ESS (e.g., hydro power plant) GWh

Variables

They are written in lower letters.

Demand

Symbol Description Unit
\(ens_n\) Energy not served GW

Generation system

Symbol Description Unit
\(gp_{ng}, gc_{ng}\) Generator output (discharge if an ESS) and consumption (charge if an ESS) GW
\(p_{ng}\) Generator output of the second block (i.e., above the minimum load) GW
\(ur_{ng}, dr_{ng}\) Upward and downward operating reserves of a committed unit GW
\(i_{ne}\) ESS stored energy (inventory) GWh
\(s_{ne}\) ESS spilled energy GWh
\(uc_{nt}, su_{nt}, sd_{nt}\) Commitment, startup and shutdown of generation unit per load level {0,1}

Equations

Objective function: minimization of operation cost for the scope of the model

Generation operation cost [M€] («eTotalTCost», «eTotalVCost», «eTotalECost»)

\[ \sum_{n}{P DUR_n \left(\sum_g {CV_g gp_{ng}} + CENS\, ens_n\right)} + \sum_{ng}{\left(DUR_n CF_g uc_{ng} + CSU_g su_{ng} + CSD_g sd_{ng}\right)} \tag{1}\]

Constraints

Balance of generation and demand [GW] («eBalance»)

\[ \sum_{g} gp_{ng} - \sum_{g} gc_{ng} + ens_n = D_n \quad \forall n \tag{2}\]

Upward and downward operating reserves [GW] («eOperReserveUp», eOperReserveDw»)

\[ \sum_g ur_{ng} \geq UR_n \quad \forall n \tag{3}\]

\[ \sum_g dr_{ng} \geq DR_n \quad \forall n \tag{4}\]

VRES units (i.e., those with linear variable cost equal to 0 and no storage capacity) do not contribute to the operating reserves.

ESS energy inventory (only for load levels multiple of 24, 168, or 672 h, depending on the ESS type) [GWh] («eESSInventory»)

\[ i_{n-\tau_e,e} + \sum_{n' = n+\nu-\tau_e}^n DUR_n (EI_{ne} - qp_{ne} + EF_e gc_{ne}) = i_{ne} + s_{ne} \quad \forall ne \tag{5}\]

Maximum and minimum output of the second block of a committed unit (all except the VRES units) [p.u.] («eMaxOutput2ndBlock», eMinOutput2ndBlock»)

  • G. Morales-España, A. Ramos, and J. Garcia-Gonzalez “An MIP Formulation for Joint Market-Clearing of Energy and Reserves Based on Ramp Scheduling” IEEE Transactions on Power Systems 29 (1): 476-488, Jan 2014. 10.1109/TPWRS.2013.2259601
  • G. Morales-España, J.M. Latorre, and A. Ramos “Tight and Compact MILP Formulation for the Thermal Unit Commitment Problem” IEEE Transactions on Power Systems 28 (4): 4897-4908, Nov 2013. 10.1109/TPWRS.2013.2251373

\[ \frac{p_{ng} + ur_{ng}}{\overline{GP}_g - \underline{GP}_g} \leq uc_{ng} \quad \forall ng \tag{6}\]

\[ \frac{p_{ng} - dr_{ng}}{\overline{GP}_g - \underline{GP}_g} \geq 0 \quad \forall ng \tag{7}\]

Total output of a committed unit (all except the VRES units) [GW] («eTotalOutput»)

\[ \frac{qp_{ng}}{\underline{GP}_g} = uc_{ng} + \frac{p_{ng}}{\underline{GP}_g} \quad \forall ng \tag{8}\]

Logical relation between commitment, startup, and shutdown status of a committed unit (all except the VRE units) [p.u.] («eUCStrShut»)

\[ uc_{ng} - uc_{n-\nu,g} = su_{ng} - sd_{ng} \quad \forall ng \tag{9}\]

Initial commitment of the units is determined by the model based on the merit order loading, including the VRES and ESS units.

Maximum ramp up and ramp down for the second block of a thermal unit [p.u.] («eRampUp», eRampDw»)

  • P. Damcı-Kurt, S. Küçükyavuz, D. Rajan, and A. Atamtürk, “A polyhedral study of production ramping,” Math. Program., vol. 158, no. 1–2, pp. 175–205, Jul. 2016. 10.1007/s10107-015-0919-9

\[ \frac{p_{nt} - p_{n-\nu,t} + ur_{nt}}{DUR_n RU_t} \leq uc_{nt} - su_{nt} \quad \forall nt \tag{10}\]

\[ \frac{p_{nt} - p_{n-\nu,t} - dr_{nt}}{DUR_n RD_t} \geq - uc_{n-\nu,t} + sd_{nt} \quad \forall nt \tag{11}\]

Minimum up time and down time of thermal unit [h] («eMinUpTime», eMinDownTime»)

  • D. Rajan and S. Takriti, “Minimum up/down polytopes of the unit commitment problem with start-up costs,” IBM, New York, Technical Report RC23628, 2005. https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/b886/42e36b414d5929fed48593d0ac46ae3e2070.pdf

\[ \sum_{n'=n+\nu-TU_t}^n su_{n't} \leq uc_{nt} \quad \forall nt \tag{12}\]

\[ \sum_{n'=n+\nu-TD_t}^n sd_{n't} \leq 1 - uc_{nt} \quad \forall nt \tag{13}\]

Bounds on generation variables [GW]

\[ 0 \leq qp_{ng} \leq \overline{GP}_g \quad \forall ng \tag{14}\]

\[ 0 \leq qc_{ne} \leq \overline{GC}_e \quad \forall ne \tag{15}\]

\[ 0 \leq ur_{ng} \leq \overline{CP}_g - \underline{GP}_g \quad \forall ng \tag{16}\]

\[ 0 \leq dr_{ng} \leq \overline{CP}_g - \underline{GP}_g \quad \forall ng \tag{17}\]

\[ 0 \leq p_{ng} \leq \overline{GP}_g - \underline{GP}_g \quad \forall ng \tag{18}\]

\[ 0 \leq i_{ne} \leq I_e \quad \forall ne \tag{19}\]

\[ 0 \leq s_{ne} \quad \forall ne \tag{20}\]

\[ 0 \leq ens_n \leq D_n \quad \forall n \tag{21}\]
